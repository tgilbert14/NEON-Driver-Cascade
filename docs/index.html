NEON Cross-Product Response Atlas

NEON measures climate and living systems across research sites in the United States. This atlas aligns several of those annual records, makes missing coverage and uncertainty visible, and screens a small set of explicitly eligible direct associations. The measurement layers are co-displayed for exploration; they are not a tested trophic chain, and the atlas does not estimate mediation or causation.

How it stays honest
A short annual record can look patterned by chance. The atlas therefore requires minimum overlap, preserves missing calendar years, uses exact finite circular-shift diagnostics for eligible direct pairings, adjusts the pooled vote family, and reports trend, outcome-estimator, and spatial sensitivity checks. The primary pooled vote currently covers two temperature-to-green-up pairings; plant and animal rows remain descriptive context because the available products do not jointly supply defensible mediators, aligned response windows, and complete effort denominators. Settings are literature-motivated and locked in the published artifact, but this is retrospective exploratory work—not a preregistration—and the hypothesis family evolved while the data were being examined. The green-up index is refit over each full snapshot, so a refresh can revise historical values; downloadable definitions and hashes preserve every published version.
