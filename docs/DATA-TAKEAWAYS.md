# NEON Driver Cascade — Data Takeaways & Critical Review

> **SUPERSEDED — July 2026 scientific audit.** The counts and conclusions below describe an older
> bundle and must not be quoted as current results. That bundle treated left-censored phenology onsets
> as exact, did not control species turnover, let outcome-conditioned beetle effort enter votes, used
> unmatched seasonal consumer responses, and framed separate pairwise links as a cascade. The rebuilt
> app uses a left-censor-screened composition-adjusted green-up index, demotes unsupported plant/animal links to
> context, adds trend sensitivities, and explicitly presents a cross-product response atlas rather than a
> tested trophic chain. Current values are generated from `data/cascade.rds` and its exports.

_Suite audit — June 2026. Synthesis of NEON DP1.10072.001 (small mammals), DP1.10003.001 (birds), DP1.10058.001 (plants), DP1.10098.001 (veg structure), DP1.10055.001 (phenology) + NEON-tower/Daymet climate._

This is the integrator app: it joins the five sibling-product signals into a per-site **annual panel** (`cascade.rds$annual`, 509 site-years × 15 columns, 46 sites, 2013–2025) and tests a bottom-up cascade — climate → green-up → producers → consumers — link by link, biome-aware, n-gated, with a cross-site pooled headline.

## What the data actually shows

- **The strongest supported pooled rung is TEMPERATURE → GREEN-UP onset** (`pooled`: from=`temp`, to=`greenup_doy`, lag=0, expected_class=`temperature-limited`). Pooled one-vote-per-site it is **23 of 32 sites sign-matching (binomial p = 0.0100, median r = −0.15)**. Warmer ⇒ earlier leaf-out. This is the suite's headline and the app leads with it correctly.
- **But no single site is statistically significant — by design.** Of those 32 testable (n≥6) `temp→greenup` sites, the strongest sign-matchers have a clean bootstrap interval (SCBI r=−0.92 CI[−0.99,−0.28]; SJER r=−0.66 CI[−1.00,−0.34]; OSBS r=−0.63 CI[−0.95,−0.16]) and so are tier `aligned`. But the circular-shift permutation floor is 1/n, so at n≤11 NO single site can reach p<0.05 (SCBI's permutation p is 0.167, its floor at n=6). The pooled binomial — not any single site — is what carries the result. The 9 counter sites (LENO +0.51, NOGP +0.51, UNDE +0.43) are the false-negative tail of small n, not refutations.
- **Producer → consumer pools to a null and the app says so.** `plant_richness→mammal_cpue` (lag 1): **22 of 40 sites match, p = 0.318, median r = 0.01** — indistinguishable from a coin flip. Richness is composition, not productivity; this is the expected null, honestly reported.
- **`precip→plant_richness` is suggestive but not significant:** 9 of 12 sites, p = 0.073, median r = 0.46. Three sites are tier `aligned` (clean CI): CLBJ r=0.85, SJER r=0.84, GUAN r=0.68. It does NOT clear 0.05 pooled, and the README correctly does not over-claim it.
- **The whole suite contains 14 `aligned` link-rows out of 460** (`suite_links`). Tier `aligned` now means sign-match AND a bootstrap interval that excludes zero — a clean per-site DIRECTION, NOT significance (no single short series can be significant under the circular-shift null's 1/n floor; significance lives only in the cross-site pooled binomial). The rest: apparent 106, counter 82, exploratory/n<6 52, insufficient 206. The honest picture is still a mostly-grey board.
- **The desert "recovery" story rests on a single site and is itself only `apparent`.** At SRER (the default site), the monsoon seed-crop prior `precip_monsoon→mammal_cpue` (lag 1) is **r = +0.72, n = 7, p = 0.060** — it does not clear 0.05, and it is the ONLY water-limited site where this expected link is even testable (n≥6). Pooled it is `1/1 site, p = 0.500`. The annual-vs-seasonal contrast is real and instructive (annual precip→rodents r=+0.20 vs monsoon r=+0.72; annual precip→richness r=−0.11 vs winter rain r=+0.27), but the winter-rain→richness recovery (+0.27) is n=5 → `exploratory`, below the verdict gate.
- **Coverage is climate-thin and desert-thin.** Annual `precip` is NA in **74%** of site-years (only 133 finite of 509); `precip_winter` 75% NA, `precip_monsoon` 71% NA, `fruiting_pct` 85% NA. The biological signals are far denser: `plant_richness` 16% NA, `mammal_mnka` 16% NA, `bird_index` 18% NA, `greenup_doy` 34% NA. So the climate driver — the bottom of the cascade — is the scarcest layer.
- **Biome mix is lopsided: 41 temperature-limited vs 5 water-limited sites** (`site_meta$biome_class`); fine classes: 25 forest, 10 grassland, 5 desert/shrub, 4 tundra/alpine, 1 subtropical dry forest, 1 other. Water-limited testable links barely exist: of the 5 water-limited sites (JORN, MOAB, ONAQ, SRER, YELL), JORN and YELL have **zero** testable expected links; the desert priors are effectively a 1-site (SRER) test.
- **Veg basal area is the credible slow-state floor** (`site_meta$veg_ba_ha`, 40 of 46 sites): **0.1 m²/ha at NOGP grassland and 0.4 at JORN Chihuahuan desert, up to 56.3 at WREF forest** — a real, directly-measured productivity gradient that plant richness (which can invert in drylands) cannot stand in for.

## How it's built

Pipeline: five sibling-app `data/sites/*.rds` bundles + the small-mammal app's monthly `data/env/*.rds` climate overlays → `scripts/build_cascade.R` → `data/cascade.rds` → the Shiny app reads the precomputed objects (no live permutation on site switch).

- **Per-product extractors** (`build_cascade.R`): `ann_env` (annual `temp` ≥8 months, `precip` ≥10 months, within-site MAD outlier NA that drops e.g. SCBI-2018's corrupted sensor year); `ann_env_seasonal` (reconstructs `precip_winter` Oct–Mar keyed to year-it-ends, `precip_monsoon` Jul–Sep, `temp_spring` Mar–May from the SAME monthly overlay); `ann_phe` (`greenup_doy` = median first-yes onset over green-up phenophases, years with ≥5 individuals); `ann_plant` (`plant_richness` species count, `plant_intro_pct`); `ann_mammal` (`mammal_cpue` = 100×captures/Nelson-&-Clark trap-nights — the flagship's CPUE definition — and `mammal_mnka`); `ann_bird` (`bird_index` = clusterSize/point, `bird_richness`); `ann_veg` (per-site `veg_ba_ha` standing stock, context not a ladder line).
- **Priors** (`priors`, 7 rows) carry expected `sign`, `lag`, `conf`, and `expected_class` (the limiting-resource regime where the mechanism is established). `site_links()` computes every prior at every site but only flags `expected=TRUE` where `expected_class` is `"all"` or matches the site's biome — that flag governs which links count toward a site's tally and which biome each pools over.
- **Stats machinery** (`cascade_helpers.R`): n-gate (<3 no compare, 3–5 exploratory no-p, ≥6 permutation p over an autocorrelation-preserving circular-shift null (response years rotated) + bootstrap CI + a tiered verdict that also requires the CI not to span 0); `pooled_links()` = binomial sign test one-vote-per-site across expected, testable sites; `signmatch_score()` = same gate, per site. `set.seed(1L)` makes it deterministic.
- **Precompute**: `suite_links` (322 rows = 46 sites × 7 priors) and `pooled` (5 rows) are baked into the bundle, so the app never recomputes 2000-permutation nulls on a site switch.

## Critical findings by lens

### NEONize (suite cohesion / gold-standard parity / honesty machinery)
- **HIGH — the desert headline over-reaches its evidence base.** The README states "the summer-monsoon seed crop drives next-year rodents at r=+0.72" as a recovered finding, and the SRER verdict sentence leads the default-site experience with it. But it is `apparent` (p=0.060, not significant), and `pooled` shows it pools across **1 site (p=0.500)**. Fix: reword to "at SRER, the one desert site where this is testable, monsoon→rodents reaches r=+0.72 (n=7, p=0.06, not yet significant) — suggestive, not established," and surface the `1/1 site` pooled count so the reader sees it is a single-site result.
- **MEDIUM — manifest/deploy hygiene flagged in `_phase23_plan.md` (B1–B3) is a recurring suite blocker.** Stale `manifest.json` checksums and the branch-mismatch (`master` vs suite-standard `main`) mean Connect Cloud can silently serve a stale bundle. Fix: wire `rsconnect::writeManifest()` into the `build_cascade.R` tail (it already attempts this at line 277) AND the CI job, and `git add manifest.json data/` together.
- **LOW — no QC-flag panel yet** (the suite gold standard per the playbook §7). The data has obvious "verify, not wrong" candidates already computed: green-up from <5 individuals, the MAD-NA'd climate years, links with CI spanning 0 shown as `apparent`. Cheap credibility win on the PI-facing capstone.

### Ecological domain
- **HIGH — the biome-scoping hides that monsoon→rodents is strongest at TEMPERATE grasslands, not deserts.** `precip_monsoon→mammal_cpue` reaches r=+0.77 (p=0.018) at KONZ and r=+0.75 (p=0.041) at CPER — both temperature-limited grasslands marked `expected=FALSE`. The mechanism (summer rain → seed crop → granivores) is biologically general to semi-arid grasslands, not a desert-only story. Defensible to a reviewer ONLY if the app notes these as out-of-prior corroboration rather than letting them sit greyed-out and uncounted. Fix: add a "tested elsewhere, not in its prior biome" callout for links that fire outside their `expected_class`.
- **MEDIUM — `temp` (annual mean) as a proxy for spring warmth is correctly caveated but `temp_spring` already exists and is denser** (379 finite vs the same MAD-filtered annual `temp` at 403). The robust pooled link uses annual `temp`; a reviewer would ask why the mechanistically-correct `temp_spring→greenup` isn't the headline test. Fix: report both, or move the pooled test to `temp_spring` where coverage allows.
- **LOW — `fruiting_pct` (85% NA, only 75 finite site-years) is too sparse to support even its weak prior.** The `fruiting_pct→mammal_cpue` link is `insufficient` (n=0) at SRER and most sites. It is honestly NA at arid sites, but consider dropping it from the headline priors list and keeping it descriptive only.

### Data science (analysis-ready / FAIR / reproducible)
- **MEDIUM — the exported codebook does not ship machine-readable column types or NA-coding.** `dlAnnual` writes a 3-line comment header then the table; `signals` carries `unit`/`higher_is`/`layer` but the CSV exports don't embed per-column NA semantics (e.g. "precip NA = <10 valid months", "fruiting NA = arid site tracks no Fruits phenophase"). Fix: emit a companion `*-codebook.csv` (key, label, layer, unit, na_meaning, n_gate) alongside each download so the export is self-describing.
- **MEDIUM — `mammal_cpue` is repeatedly (and correctly) labelled a within-site index, but the suite scoreboard CSV (`dlSuite`) pools sites on links built from it** without restating that caveat in the file header. Anyone reading `neon-cascade-scoreboard.csv` standalone could read cross-site. Fix: add the within-site-index caveat to the `dlSuite` and `dlLinks` headers too (only `dlAnnual` has it).
- **LOW — provenance/version is thin.** `meta` has `built_when` (2026-06-19) and a prose `built` string but no NEON release tag or sibling-bundle hashes. For reproducibility, stamp the sibling-bundle build dates into `meta`.

### Statistics
- **HIGH — small-n honesty is the design's strength, but the pooled table mixes a real result with two-and-one-site "pools."** `pooled` row 5 (`precip_monsoon→mammal_cpue`, sites=1, k=1, p=0.500) is a binomial on a single vote — it is not a pooled test at all and should be flagged or suppressed, not displayed in the same headline list as the 32-site temp→greenup result. Fix: gate `pooled_links()` to require sites≥3 (or ≥5) before reporting a pooled p, and show under-floor links as "1 site — not poolable."
- **MEDIUM — the `aligned` tier can be assigned to a link outside its expected biome** (SJER `precip_winter→plant_richness` r=0.88 CI[0.72,1.00] is `aligned` but `expected=FALSE`). The tier word is biome-blind; only the tally respects `expected`. A reader scanning tier colors could miscount. Mitigation already shipped: out-of-prior cells are dimmed in the scoreboard and carry a corner mark.
- **LOW — permutation/binomial/lag mechanics are valid and should not be touched** (confirmed: shuffle-the-response null, ≥6-year gate, CI-spans-zero guard, one-vote-per-site binomial). The honest move is upstream: more seasonal coverage and more water-limited sites, not more stats.

## Honest-stats & caveats — what this app must NOT be read to claim

- **Not "rain drives desert rodents."** The desert cascade is a **single-site, non-significant** observation (SRER monsoon→rodents r=+0.72, p=0.060) plus an n=5 exploratory richness recovery. It is a compelling illustration of the annual-aggregation artifact, NOT an established result. The 5-water-limited-site sample cannot support a desert generalization.
- **Not "the cascade holds."** Only 14 of 460 link-rows are `aligned` (a clean per-site direction, not significance); only ONE rung (temp→greenup) survives cross-site pooling at p<0.05, and significance is pooled-only. The producer→consumer rung pools to a null (p=0.318).
- **Not productivity.** `plant_richness` is composition and can invert in drylands; `veg_ba_ha` is the real productivity floor but is a ~5-year STATE, not an annual link.
- **Not cross-site abundance.** `mammal_cpue` and `bird_index` are within-site relative indices; cross-site magnitude comparisons are invalid (only the sign-match pooling, which discards magnitude, is legitimate across sites).
- **Climate is the thinnest layer (74% of `precip` site-years NA),** so many "insufficient" verdicts (157 of 169 are n=0) are coverage gaps at the bottom of the cascade, not weak ecology — the grey board is largely a data-availability statement.

## Place in the cascade

This is the synthesis node: it consumes the sibling signals and is the only app that can answer "does bottom-up cascade theory hold across NEON?" Its verified answer: **one rung holds (climate→green-up, pooled p=0.010), the rest are too short per-site and pool to null or near-null.** It corroborates the suite-wide truth that the climate→green-up link is the durable one, demonstrates that desert "failures" are an annual-aggregation artifact (seasonal split flips SRER precip→richness −0.11→+0.27 and precip→rodents +0.20→+0.72), and honestly exposes that the producer→consumer rung — the top of the bottom-up chain — does not resolve at NEON's current series lengths. Its most fundable contribution is the **biome-conditional priors framing**: the right driver depends on whether warmth or water limits the system, and testing the wrong one is what made the desert look empty.
